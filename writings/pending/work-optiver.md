# Optiver Services B.V.

May '23 - Jun '23

Quantitative Trader Intern

## Website copy

- Completed Optiver's structured trading training programme covering the full spectrum of options theory; from Black-Scholes and volatility to Greeks interpretation and advanced option structures.
- Traded EURO STOXX 50 options across strikes and expiries spanning the full 8-hour trading day for 3 weeks, dynamically managing Greeks, intraday risk, and event-driven volatility.
- Led a quantitative research project analysing event-day inefficiencies on Single-Stock-Options, quantifying a potential EUR 5 million annual opportunity loss and proposing a statistically fitted execution-width model that was later adopted as an intra-desk optimisation framework.

## Long write-up

Optiver is a psychological battle. You walk in hyper-aware of the talent density and can't help but feel like you're the dumbest person in the room. All the time. 

I would try hard all day to stay alert during theory. Try to understand what's going on when I knew I had no clue. Solving exercises with the others was fun. Playing trading games was fun. Picking the minds of literal geniuses was fun. But staying awake in class was so not it. They should hand out nootropics and 3 cans of redbull a day to interns if they wanna sustain the same training model

Simulated trading was fun. I had no idea what to do on the first day because I fell asleep during the briefing. My eyes were open but I was asleep. I clicked a bunch of buttons thinking fuck it, we ball. I caught up on what all of it meant with my friends in the evening that day. After that trading was just make money, don't lose money. Don't fake the money by manipulating the vol estimate. And glaze Prateek Prateek just cuz his name was Prateek Prateek (And he was a good trader). I just did what I thought made sense in my gut. I had no idea what I was doing. I apparently made a lot of fake money.

The research project was alright. It seemed open-ended and if something is open-ended and the deadline is 3 weeks I'll face paralysis. Especially when I'm already dealing with impostor syndrome, a complete lack of bitches, poor sleep from trying things legal in amsterdam and a consciousness stuck in survival mode. I wrote a lot of code, made a lot of charts, didn't understand anything. My project guide came over on the last day, saw the data and charts and told me the insights and said I should be proud. Lies. I also broke my up-down desk cuz I pushed down too hard. 

The breakfast spread is world-class, the game area is sick, the people are terrifying and awe-inspiring. Friday evening drinks are fun. Amsterdam is really cool, lives up to the hype. Didn't indulge in red-light activities (But if I did, I'd still deny it). Visited Belgium too. Made some friends, who may or may not stay so though we'ren't in touch.
