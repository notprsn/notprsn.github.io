# Writing Quality Rubric

This rubric adapts the main editing principles from Adebola Williams' Medium article on writing well and translates them into a reusable review checklist for this repo.

Use it when you need to judge whether a draft is merely written or actually ready.

## The five checks

### 1. Clarity

The reader should understand what the piece is saying without decoding it.

Questions:
- Can the main point be stated in one sentence?
- Does each paragraph clearly help that point?
- Are there lines that sound impressive but say little?

Red flags:
- vague abstractions
- throat-clearing intros
- long sentences doing too many jobs

### 2. Coherence

The piece should move with intent. One idea should lead to the next.

Questions:
- Does the opening set up what follows?
- Do transitions feel earned rather than abrupt?
- Would the order still make sense if read aloud?

Red flags:
- smart-sounding fragments that do not connect
- repeated points in different words
- sudden tonal or topic jumps

### 3. Consistent Tone

Voice is part of the value. Editing should sharpen it, not erase it.

Questions:
- Does the piece sound like one person throughout?
- Is the level of formality stable?
- Are humor, bluntness, tenderness, or swagger used deliberately?

Red flags:
- switching between personal essay voice and resume voice
- generic "content" phrasing
- over-sanitizing lines that gave the draft its personality

### 4. Language

Use the right words, not the fanciest ones.

Questions:
- Are verbs active and specific?
- Is the grammar clean enough not to distract?
- Are there filler phrases that can be cut with no loss?

Red flags:
- repeated intensifiers
- clunky noun piles
- lines that need to be read twice to parse

### 5. Emotional or Logical Effect

The piece should leave the reader with either a clear idea, a feeling, or both.

Questions:
- Does the piece teach, reveal, persuade, or move?
- Is there a memorable image, scene, or conclusion?
- After reading, does anything stick?

Red flags:
- technically clean but emotionally flat prose
- personal writing with no actual insight
- analytical writing with no clear takeaway

## Fast scoring

Rate each category from 1 to 5.

- `1`: weak, confusing, or missing
- `3`: workable but uneven
- `5`: sharp and publication-ready

Do not finalize a piece if:
- clarity is below `4`
- coherence is below `4`
- the factual confidence for a `work-*` or `projects-*` draft is below `4`

## Editing stance

- Clarity beats cleverness.
- Strong voice beats generic polish.
- Specific detail beats inflated phrasing.
- A piece can be rough at the sentence level and still have a strong core. Fix the core first.
