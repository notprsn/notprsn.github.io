# Editing Passes

Use this sequence when actively revising a draft. It combines repo needs with a few durable writing habits: outline the piece, draft before micro-editing, and read the final version aloud.

## 1. Intent pass

Write down:
- who the piece is for
- what it is trying to say
- what feeling or takeaway should remain at the end

If you cannot answer those quickly, the draft is not ready for line edits.

## 2. Structure pass

Build or revise a simple outline:
- opening hook, scene, or direct claim
- body sections in a sensible order
- ending that lands the point instead of fading out

Moves to make:
- cut duplicate paragraphs
- group related material together
- move the strongest concrete detail earlier
- start with story when the piece feels too abstract

## 3. Development pass

Strengthen the content inside the structure.

For essays:
- turn general thoughts into scenes, moments, or observations
- keep only the details that earn their place

For `work-*` and `projects-*` drafts:
- keep claims factual and specific
- prefer outcomes, responsibilities, and constraints over hype
- if a metric or detail is uncertain, flag it instead of embellishing

## 4. Line-edit pass

Now tighten the prose.

Targets:
- shorter, cleaner sentences
- stronger verbs
- fewer filler phrases
- less repetition
- cleaner transitions

Useful cuts:
- "I think"
- "in a way"
- "basically"
- stacked qualifiers that weaken the sentence

## 5. Read-aloud pass

Read the piece in the intended voice.

Listen for:
- awkward rhythm
- buried punchlines or insights
- sentences that look fine but sound wrong
- places where energy drops

If a sentence is hard to say, rewrite it.

## 6. Markdown pass

Convert the draft into clean site-ready markdown:
- one `#` title
- headings only where they help
- bullets only when they improve scanability
- links only when worth clicking

## 7. Ship or hold

Ship when:
- the core point is obvious
- the structure feels intentional
- the voice still sounds like the author
- the ending lands

Hold when:
- the draft still depends on missing facts
- the opening does not earn attention
- the piece says the same thing three times
